Mum's Origami
She takes her soul, her body,
her mind. Mixes them up,
makes a paper out of it.
A blank one, she binds.
She lays it down flat
focuses her full heart at
the paper lying low.
She straighten up few corners
gives it a little blow.
She starts folding now
with her stained hands.
Dirt gets printed on the paper
with every fingertip she lands
Now paper has own stains
stains of its own mind
and its own thoughts
and the dirt has her traces
of the seed from her the paper got.
She moves to the next side.
Makes few slides here and there.
A few openings she shuts.
But she doesn't even care
about so many paper cuts of her own.
As she finishes the piece
she smiles with satisfaction
at the piece, me, her fraction.
She kisses and looks at me
at her I look, I, my mum's origami.
